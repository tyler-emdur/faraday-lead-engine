## Channel 1: Public Adjusters (PAs)
**Rank: #1 — Highest priority, fastest path to warm leads**

### Why this works
PAs are licensed to negotiate insurance claims on behalf of homeowners. They work on commission — typically 10–15% of the claim payout. They already have clients with documented hail damage sitting in their pipeline right now. They need a roofing contractor who is fast, professional, and makes them look good. One good PA relationship = 3–10 leads over a season with zero ongoing effort.

A contractor in Texas reported a 40% rise in referrals after building a PA network. The relationship is simple: PA negotiates the claim, Faraday does the roof. Both get paid.

### How to find them
Colorado DORA maintains a public roster of every licensed PA in the state. Download it here:  
**https://apps2.colorado.gov/dora/licensing/lookup/generateroster.aspx**  
Filter by "Public Adjuster." There are ~200–400 licensed PAs in Colorado. Focus on Denver metro, Aurora, Lakewood, Fort Collins, Colorado Springs.

### How to approach
**Call, do not email.** PAs are field workers. They don't sit at desks. They answer their phones.

Script:
> "Hey [name], my name's Tyler, I'm with Faraday Construction. We specialize in insurance restoration roofing on the Front Range — we handle all the supplements, attend the adjuster meetings, the whole thing. I wanted to reach out because we've been looking for a PA to partner with in [city]. Do you have 5 minutes?"

If yes:
> "We want to be your go-to contractor when your clients need a roofer. We turn around estimates in 24 hours, we've never missed a supplement, and we make the whole process easy for you. Is there a way we can work together?"

### What you're asking for
Not a contract. Not a formal agreement. Just: "When your client needs a roofer, send them our way." Give them the landing page URL and Tyler's cell.

### Effort
3 hours to pull and filter the DORA list. 1–2 hours calling 20 PAs. Realistically reach 8–10, get 2–4 interested.

**Cost:** $0  
**Time to first lead:** 3–7 days  
**Expected leads (60 days):** 8–12 from 3 active PA partners  
**Likelihood:** High. Both parties have direct financial incentive.

## Channel 2: Google Business Profile
**Rank: #2 — Free, set-once, generates leads while you sleep**

### Why this works
60–90% of roofing leads come directly from Google's local map pack — the 3 businesses shown when someone searches "roofer near me" or "hail damage roof inspection Denver." This is free. A properly set-up GBP starts appearing in the map pack within 2 weeks.

### What to do
1. Go to business.google.com → Create a profile for Faraday Construction
2. Enter a Denver-area service address (can be a P.O. Box or Faraday's office)
3. Set service area: all Front Range zip codes
4. Category: "Roofing Contractor"
5. Add phone number: (720) 766-1518
6. Website: leads.faradaysun.com
7. Upload 5–10 photos of real jobs
8. Add services: "Free Hail Damage Inspection," "Insurance Claim Assistance," "Roof Replacement"
9. Write a 200-word description mentioning hail, insurance, Colorado, Front Range

That's it. Takes 45 minutes.

### What happens next
Within 2 weeks the profile starts appearing. Every week, add one Google Post (a photo + caption about storm damage or a recent job). This signals to Google that the profile is active and improves ranking.

**Cost:** $0  
**Time to first lead:** 10–14 days  
**Expected leads (60 days):** 4–8  
**Likelihood:** High. This is how most homeowners find contractors. No reason not to do it today.

## Channel 3: Thumbtack
**Rank: #3 — Lowest cost paid lead platform**

### Why this works
Among the lead platforms, Thumbtack has the lowest true cost per booked job at ~$250 vs. Angi's $542. Homeowners post jobs ("I need a roof inspection after recent hail") and contractors quote them. You only pay when you choose to quote a lead — no subscription required.

A free Thumbtack listing also shows up when homeowners browse the category, even if you're not actively quoting.

### What to do
1. Create a free Thumbtack profile at thumbtack.com/pro
2. Category: Roofing
3. Add photos, description, and services
4. Turn on "Instant Match" for hail damage and roof inspection jobs in Denver metro
5. Set a budget of $30–50/week if Faraday will pay — this gets you in front of 3–5 active homeowner requests per week

### Important note
Do not pay for Angi Pro leads. They sell the same lead to 3–5 contractors simultaneously. Homeowners get flooded with calls and often ignore everyone. Angi's cost per booked job is $542 vs. Thumbtack's $250.

**Cost:** $0 for listing; $30–50/week if paying for quotes (Faraday should pay this, not Tyler)  
**Time to first lead:** 1–3 days  
**Expected leads (60 days):** 3–5  
**Likelihood:** Moderate. Real homeowners with real intent. Competitive but manageable.

## Channel 4: Nextdoor
**Rank: #4 — Highest trust, hyperlocal, free**

### Why this works
Nextdoor is neighborhood-specific. A recommendation from a neighbor carries more weight than any ad. Homeowners regularly post "does anyone know a good roofer?" after storms — and contractors who are active in the neighborhood get recommended. It's also free to create a business profile and post.

### What to do
1. Create a free Nextdoor Business Profile at business.nextdoor.com
2. Set service areas: Denver, Aurora, Lakewood, Westminster, Fort Collins, Colorado Springs
3. Post once per week in relevant neighborhoods: a short note about storm damage, a before/after photo, or a helpful tip about hail damage timelines

**Organic post template (post this personally in your neighborhood or neighborhoods where you live):**
> "Heads up for neighbors — a lot of homes in [neighborhood] have hail damage from the [month] storms that isn't visible from the ground. Insurance usually covers it completely — you only pay your deductible. Faraday Construction is doing free inspections this week. Happy to answer questions here or you can check out leads.faradaysun.com."

4. When someone asks for a roofer recommendation anywhere in the area — reply. Be helpful, not salesy.

**Cost:** $0  
**Time to first lead:** 1–7 days after first post (if post gets traction)  
**Expected leads (60 days):** 3–6  
**Likelihood:** Moderate-high. Neighborhood trust is the highest-converting referral source in home services.

## Channel 6: Home Inspectors
**Rank: #6 — Strong referral potential, requires right approach**

### Why this works
Home inspectors do 3–5 inspections per day. They flag roof issues on the majority of inspections in hail-prone Colorado. They can't officially recommend a single contractor (ASHI/InterNACHI ethics rules say they should provide a list, not a name), but in practice many inspectors have preferred contractors they mention informally.

The best angle: mutual referral. Inspectors refer homeowners to Faraday; Faraday refers buyers who need an inspection to the inspector. Both benefit.

### How to approach
**Call**, not email. Same logic as PAs — inspectors are in the field.

Script:
> "Hi [name], I'm Tyler with Faraday Construction, we do roofing and hail damage claims on the Front Range. I wanted to reach out because I know you're flagging roof issues on inspections regularly and I'd love to be someone you can point homeowners toward when that happens — and I'd be happy to refer buyers your way who need inspections. Do you have 2 minutes?"

What you're asking for: "When a homeowner asks you who they should call about their roof, would you mention Faraday?" That's it.

**Do not** pitch a formal referral fee arrangement — it can create ethics issues for inspectors. Keep it informal and mutual.

**Cost:** $0  
**Time to first lead:** 7–21 days  
**Expected leads (60 days):** 2–4 from 2 active inspector relationships  
**Likelihood:** Moderate. Requires finding inspectors who are open to informal referrals.

## Channel 8: Restoration Contractors
**Rank: #8 — Underused referral source**

### Why this works
Water damage restoration companies (ServPro franchises, BELFOR, local shops) go into homes after flooding. They frequently see roof damage as the source of the water intrusion. They currently have no roofing partner to refer to — and their customers need roofing urgently.

### How to find them
Search Google Maps: "water damage restoration Denver" — you'll find 20–30 local companies.

### How to approach
Same phone approach:
> "Hi, I'm Tyler from Faraday Construction. We do roofing and insurance claims. I know you're often finding roof damage as the source of leaks — would you be open to referring those homeowners our way? We'd do the same for you."

**Cost:** $0  
**Time to first lead:** 7–21 days  
**Expected leads (60 days):** 2–4  
**Likelihood:** Moderate. Less competitive than PAs, good lead quality.
